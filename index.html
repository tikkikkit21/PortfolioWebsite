My first interaction with computer science was in 5th grade when my dad introduced to me to Scratch, a drag-and-drop language designed for kids. I messed around with it and ended up with a starfish flying across the screen while screaming. I soon forgot about it until my freshman year of high school. I joined my school's FRC (FIRST Robotics Competition) club since I enjoyed STEM and it sounded cool. I originally wanted to join the build team, but I ended up being forced to do programming since they were short-handed. Since I didn't have much programming experience, I spent the season trying to learn how to code without actually contributing much. As a result, I took AP Computer Science my sophmore year in order to actually learn a language. It was during this class that I fell in love with programming. Despite only learning the very basics of Java, I was amazed at all the different possibilities computer science had to offer. If I had an idea, I could code it. My love soon developed into a fiery passion for CS.

Today, I'm pursuing that passion. I'm currently a second-year at Virginia Tech, pursuing a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science. While I enjoy backend development the most, I aim to be a well-rounded programmer. That's why for my undergraduate, I'm taking electives like Cloud Software Dev, Mobile Dev, Graphics, and Web Dev. After graduation, I plan to pursue a Master's of Engineering in CS, where I'll be focusing on Artificial Intelligence and Data Analytics. From there, I would like to pursue a career in autonomy with vechicles and/or robotics.
